Focal Length

Same scene, same pose — only the lens changes. Watch background compression increase and subject isolation grow.
8mm Fisheye
180° barrel distortion, everything curved, skate/music video
24mm Wide
More environment, barrel distortion, subject feels "in the world"
50mm Normal
Natural perspective, closest to human eye, clean neutral look
85mm Portrait
Background softens, flattering compression, subject isolated
135mm Telephoto
Heavy compression, creamy bokeh, voyeuristic/paparazzi feel
⟁

Camera Angle — Vertical

Same framing, same lens — only the vertical angle changes. Controls power dynamics: low = powerful, high = vulnerable.
Worm's Eye
Ground level looking up, extreme power
Low Angle
Subject dominant, threatening, hero shot
Eye Level
Neutral, documentary, equal relationship
High Angle
Subject diminished, vulnerable, exposed
Bird's Eye
God view, omniscient, graphic composition
⟁

Camera Angle — Horizontal

Camera rotates around the subject. Changes how much of the face is revealed and the relationship between viewer and character.
Frontal
Direct address, confrontational, Kubrick
Three-Quarter
Most common portrait angle, natural
Profile / Side
90° side view, graphic, editorial
Three-Quarter Rear
Mystery, partial concealment
Rear / Behind
Following, voyeuristic, journey
Over-the-Shoulder
POV-adjacent, dialogue standard
⟁

Camera Angle — Dutch / Tilt

Same shot, only the horizon tilts. Subtle tilt = unease. Extreme tilt = chaos and disorientation.
Level Horizon
Stable, neutral, standard
Slight Dutch
Subtle unease, tension
Extreme Dutch
Disorientation, horror, chaos
◐

Lighting Direction

Same close-up portrait — only light source direction changes. The single biggest factor in visual mood.
Front Lit
Flat, even, commercial/beauty
Rembrandt
Triangle on cheek, classic dramatic portrait
Backlit
Silhouette, rim light, mysterious
Top Light
Godfather style, eyes in shadow, menacing
Under Light
Horror, unnatural upward shadows
◧

Color Grading

Same medium shot, same scene — only the color grade changes. Completely shifts emotional tone without touching anything physical.
Warm Amber
Nostalgic, hopeful, Spielberg golden hour
Teal / Cyan
Cold, clinical, thriller tension (Fincher)
Desaturated
Gritty realism, war film aesthetic
Teal & Orange
Hollywood blockbuster standard
Black & White
Timeless, graphic, art house
❖

Genre Aesthetic

Same character — genre keyword triggers an entire visual system. One word changes everything: lighting, color, composition, mood.
Spy Thriller
Cool tones, urban, anamorphic, MI4 vibe
Film Noir
B&W, hard shadows, rain, wet streets
Cyberpunk
Neon pink/blue, rain, saturated
Romantic Drama
Golden hour, warm, soft, intimate
Horror
Wide angle, under-lit, dutch angle, dread
🎬

Director Styles

Same hotel corridor, same person — different director name triggers a completely different visual language.
Kubrick
Symmetry, one-point perspective, cold precision, 4:3
Fincher
Teal, crushed blacks, low-key, clinical
Wong Kar-wai
Saturated neon, motion blur, romantic melancholy
Wes Anderson
Centered, pastel, symmetrical, whimsical, 4:3

Camera Movement — Stabilization Type

Same scene — only the camera stabilization changes. This defines the physical "feel" of the footage. 🎥 Video clips.
Locked Off / Tripod
Perfectly static, zero movement, controlled
Handheld
Organic shake, urgency, documentary
Steadicam
Smooth floating glide, Kubrick hallways
Gimbal Stabilized
Ultra-smooth modern, YouTube/commercial
⟿

Camera Movement — Direction

Same subject — only the camera's movement direction changes. This is the core of video cinematography. 🎥 Video clips.
Push In / Dolly In
Moving toward subject, increasing intimacy
Pull Out / Dolly Out
Moving away, revealing context
Tracking Left/Right
Lateral following movement
Tracking Backward
Subject walks toward camera, hero walk
Crane Up
Rising vertical, reveal or triumph
Orbit / Arc
Circling around subject, dramatic
Whip Pan
Fast horizontal blur transition
⟿

Camera Movement — Speed

Same movement direction — only the speed changes. Slow = tension. Fast = shock. 🎥 Video clips.
Slow Creep
Barely perceptible, tension building
Steady Pace
Matching subject walk speed
Crash Zoom / Snap
Sudden violent movement, shock
🏃

Subject Motion — Character Action

Same character, same environment — only what the subject does changes. This is the other half of video prompting. 🎥 Video clips.
Standing Still
Minimal motion, breathing, portrait feel
Walking Toward Camera
Confident approach, power, presence
Walking Away
Departure, mystery, leaving behind
Turning Head
Dramatic reveal, awareness, slow turn
Running
Urgency, chase, action energy
Sitting, Subtle Gestures
Calm, contemplative, intimate
🌧

Subject Motion — Environmental

Same subject standing still — only the environment moves. Adds atmosphere and life to otherwise static shots. 🎥 Video clips.
Rain Falling
Atmosphere, noir, melancholy, wet reflections
Wind Blowing
Dynamic, outdoor energy, hair/clothes movement
Crowd Moving
Isolation in crowd, urban life, loneliness
Neon Flickering
Colored light pulsing on face, cyberpunk
🔄

Transitions

How to connect two shots in a single prompt using "Shot 1" and "Shot 2" structure. Each transition type changes the emotional feel between scenes. 🎥 Video clips.
Hard Cut
Instant sharp scene change, no blending
Dissolve / Crossfade
Soft blend between two scenes, dreamy
Whip Pan Transition
Fast blur connecting two different locations
